To formulate a simulated or functional plant-based Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) directly from your un-centrifuged blue corn and lime slurry, the primary objective is to dilute the heavy starch and lipid concentration down to match the highly aqueous, electrolyte-dense profile of natural CSF. Natural CSF is over 99% water, with trace proteins, glucose, and a highly specific ionic balance.

For an "athletic" variant---which typically optimizes for high-conductivity electrolyte retention and stable osmotic pressure---the raw slurry must be significantly extended with distilled water to prevent it from remaining a thick paste or heavy emulsion.

1\. Volumetric Formula and Ratios

Because the slurry has not been centrifuged, it contains all the suspended starches, fibers, and lipids. To bring the viscosity and density down to a water-like fluid baseline while keeping the bioactive nutrients in suspension, use a **1:9 to 1:12 dilution ratio** of raw slurry to distilled water.

-   **Raw Un-Centrifuged Slurry (Hot, cooked):** 1 Part by volume (e.g., 100 mL)
-   **Distilled Water (Deionized):** 10 Parts by volume (e.g., 1,000 mL or 1.0 L)

Resulting Mass Balance of the Fluid:

-   **Total Aqueous Phase:** ~98.5%
-   **Suspended Plant Lipids/Proteins:** ~1.0%
-   **Dissolved Ions & Corn Carbohydrates:** ~0.5%

* * * * *

2\. Step-by-Step Formulation Process

```
[Hot Slurry (1 Part)] + [Distilled Water (10 Parts)] -> [High-Shear Blending] -> [Rapid Cooling (<4°C)]

```

1.  **Thermal Stabilization:** Ensure the distilled water is pre-heated to approximately **50°C to 60°C** before mixing. Adding cold water to the hot corn slurry will cause the starches to immediately gelatinize into dense, clumpy aggregates ("jelly"), ruining the fluid consistency.
2.  **High-Shear Blending:** Combine the hot slurry and distilled water. Pass the mixture through a high-shear inline mixer or an industrial colloid mill. Because there is no centrifuge step to separate the layers, mechanical shear is the only way to force the corn lipids and starches into a permanent micro-emulsion that won't immediately settle.
3.  **Electrolyte Adjustment (Optional for "Athletic" Profile):** To properly match the high sodium/chloride content of athletic performance fluids and natural CSF, add **0.9 grams of pure Sodium Chloride (NaCl)** per 100 mL of total fluid volume.

* * * * *

3\. Stability and Fermentation Control (No-Centrifuge Challenges)

Skipping the centrifuge leaves all the fermentable sugars and starches fully exposed in a highly diluted aqueous environment. This creates an ideal breeding ground for rapid, illegal wild fermentation.

-   **Viscosity Control:** The starches will naturally want to settle to the bottom over time. To keep the fluid completely homogeneous without a centrifuge, you may need to add a food-grade stabilizer like **Xanthan Gum (0.05% by weight)** during the blending phase to keep the micro-particles suspended indefinitely.
-   **Immediate Thermal Shock:** Because of the high water activity (\(a_{w}\)) of this diluted mixture, it must be run through a plate heat exchanger to drop its temperature from **96°C to under 4°C in less than 30 seconds** immediately after blending to prevent airborne yeasts from activating.
