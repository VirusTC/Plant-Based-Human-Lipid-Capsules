To optimize the extraction of lipids from blue corn grits using lime juice while managing runoff and avoiding illegal fermentation, you must balance the thermal kinetics and mass ratios. This process relies on acidic nixtamalization principles to break down the corn endosperm matrix and release structural triglycerides.

1\. Optimal Input and Mass Ratios
---------------------------------

To handle excessive runoff and ensure complete saturation of the grits without drowning the mixture, use a standard 1:3 weight-to-volume ratio for the initial mix, adjusting based on target moisture absorption.

-   Blue Corn Grits: 1.0 kg (Baseline dry weight)
-   Lime Juice (Pure, filtered): 3.0 L (approx. 3.15 kg)
-   Target Runoff Management: Corn grits absorb roughly 100% to 120% of their dry weight in liquid during extended cooking. Anticipate approximately 1.8 Liters of runoff juice post-centrifugation, which can be recycled for subsequent extractions after filtering out suspended starches.

2\. Step-by-Step Thermal and Processing Sequence
------------------------------------------------

```
[Heat Components Separately] -> [Combine & Flash Heat] -> [Simmer & Agitate] -> [Phase Separation]
  (Lime: 66.67°C / Corn: 80°C)          (96.34°C)             (3 Hours)          (Centrifuge)

```

Step 1: Pre-Heating Phase
-------------------------

-   Action: Heat the filtered lime juice to exactly 66.67°C. Concurrently, dry-heat or steam-condition the blue corn grits to 80.00°C.
-   Purpose: Pre-heating prevents thermal shock, minimizes starch retrogradation, and ensures the mixture immediately enters the optimal lipid-release window upon contact.

Step 2: High-Thermal Extraction Mix
-----------------------------------

-   Action: Combine the pre-heated grits and lime juice. Immediately raise the temperature of the combined slurry to 96.34°C.
-   Purpose: This ultra-high thermal threshold disrupts the protein-starch complexes (zein proteins) enclosing the lipid droplets.

Step 3: Controlled Cook and Agitation
-------------------------------------

-   Action: Maintain the slurry at 96.34°C for 3 hours. Stir continuously or provide low-shear mechanical agitation.
-   Purpose: Prolonged exposure to heat and low pH hydrolyzes cell wall components, lowering the viscosity of the interstitial space and liberating trapped triglycerides.

Step 4: Rapid Centrifugal Separation
------------------------------------

-   Action: Separate the liquid runoff from the solids immediately while hot (above 60°C) using a solid-bowl centrifuge.
-   Settings: Spin at 3,000 to 4,500 RPM for 15 minutes.
-   Outcome: Three distinct layers will form: a heavy starch/protein pellet at the bottom, an aqueous lime juice runoff layer in the middle, and a pure, hydrophobic lipid/oil layer at the top.

Prevention of Illegal Fermentation
----------------------------------

To prevent the extracted sugar-rich "jelly" or runoff from fermenting into an unmarketable/illegal alcoholic byproduct, you must arrest microbial activity immediately post-extraction:

-   Thermal Kill Step: Flash pasteurize the separated components at 72°C for 15 seconds or 85°C for 3 minutes.
-   Cooling Rate: Cool the separated fractions to below 4°C within 1 hour using a heat exchanger or ice bath to suppress wild yeasts.
-   Brix / pH Monitoring: Keep the pH below 3.5 using the natural lime acidity to inhibit bacterial growth, and check the Brix level to track total dissolved sugars available for potential fermentation.
