1\. Material Genesis & Molecular Integration

All structural organic matter on Earth originates through primary biological synthesis. According to the law of **Conservation of Mass**, matter cannot be created or destroyed; it can only change forms. The elemental building blocks of human tissue---carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen---are assembled by plant life using solar energy, water, and atmospheric carbon dioxide.

When humans consume nutrients, they participate in this continuous cycle:

```
[Solar Energy + CO₂ + H₂O] ──> [Primary Plant Synthesis] ──> [Animal/Human Consumption] ──> [Cellular Assimilation]

```

Whether a system digests plant matter directly or utilizes animal products from livestock that consumed forage, every organic molecule is tracking back to a botanical origin.

This product delivers isolated, unrefined agricultural lipid structures directly to an external boundary interface. When applied topically to the skin's surface, these direct plant molecules, neutral triglycerides, and plant wax structures sit within the intercellular spaces of the stratum corneum. They function as a temporary, protective external barrier layer until the components are gradually shed, absorbed, or replaced through the epidermis's natural desquamation and regenerative cycles.

3\. Conservation of Mass in External Fluid Dynamics

The stability of this topical barrier relies strictly on physical mass balances. In an enclosed environment or on a localized dermal surface, the input weight of the distilled water carrier and raw lipids must equal the final output weight of the set barrier cream plus any ambient moisture evaporation. This high-moisture distilled water base and fresh plant oil mixture is formulated to deliver external hydration to skin tissues, shielding the body's natural moisture boundary from environmental drying.
